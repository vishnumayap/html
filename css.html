UI/UX DESIGN

User Interface (UI) refers to the visual elements and overall look of a digital product. It involves designing the layout, aesthetics, and presentation of the user interface, including buttons, icons, menus, and other graphical elements. The UI designer focuses on creating an appealing and visually cohesive interface that is easy to understand and navigate.
User Experience (UX), on the other hand, encompasses the overall experience and interaction that users have with a digital product or service. It involves understanding the users' needs, goals, and behaviors and designing the product in a way that is intuitive, efficient, and enjoyable to use. UX designers conduct research, create user personas, develop user flows, and design wireframes and prototypes to ensure a smooth and satisfying user experience.
